# Ecofin Editorial Policy

**Version:** 0.1
**Status:** Active
**Last updated:** 04.07.2026

Ecofin is an open-source financial literacy company focused on Uzbekistan. Our goal is to explain personal finance, economics, credit, savings, inflation, Islamic finance, and related topics in a practical, local, and understandable way.

We write for ordinary people first. Our content should help readers make better decisions, avoid harmful financial behavior, understand financial products, and think more clearly about money. **The focus is always on the reader**.

Ecofin is not a neutral encyclopedia. We are an opinionated educational company guided by practical usefulness, transparency, and Islamic finance principles.

---

## 1. Editorial Mission

Ecofin exists to improve financial literacy in Uzbekistan.

We publish articles, series, calculators, graphs, and educational tools that explain financial topics clearly and practically. Our priority is not to publish the most content, but to publish content that is useful, accurate, and relevant to real financial decisions for ordinary Uzbek families.

Our editorial work is built around several principles:

1. **Local relevance**
   We explain finance in the context of Uzbekistan: local banks, local salaries, local inflation, local habits, local laws, and local financial risks.

2. **Practical value**
   We do not write only definitions. Every article should help the reader understand what to do, what to avoid, what to calculate, or what questions to ask.

3. **Clarity**
   We avoid unnecessary jargon. When technical terms are necessary, we explain them in simple language.

4. **Transparency**
   We show our assumptions, formulas, sources, and reasoning wherever possible.

5. **Ethical finance**
   Ecofin does not promote betting, gambling, speculative trading, exploitative debt, deceptive financial products, or interest-based income as a moral ideal. Islamic finance is our guide.

6. **Open knowledge**
   We believe high-quality financial education should be freely available. The business model is built differently.

## 2. Scope of Coverage

Ecofin may publish content on economics, finance, and technology, notably:

- personal budgeting;
- saving and emergency funds;
- debt and credit;
- consumer loans;
- mortgages;
- inflation;
- exchange rates;
- gold and other stores of value;
- bank deposits;
- financial planning;
- Islamic finance;
- taxes and personal financial obligations;
- financial scams and fraud prevention;
- economics explained for ordinary readers;
- financial careers, certifications, and education;
- financial tools, calculators, and open data.

Ecofin may also publish institutional or career-focused series about universities, companies, internships, certifications, and professional pathways, as long as the content is educational and clearly disclosed.

Ecofin does not publish promotional investment tips, betting strategies, day-trading signals, guaranteed-return schemes, or content designed to push readers into risky financial behavior. This is beyond our border.

---

## 3. Islamic Finance Position

Ecofin aims to follow Islamic finance principles.

We do not encourage, endorse, or advertise financial practices that we understand to be impermissible under Islamic finance principles, including:

- riba/interest-based exploitation;
- betting and gambling;
- speculative trading presented as a path to wealth;
- guaranteed income without risk-sharing;
- deceptive accounting;
- financial pyramids;
- financial products designed to hide harm behind technical language.

At the same time, Ecofin is an educational project, not a fatwa institution. We do not claim final religious authority. When discussing Islamic finance, we aim to distinguish between:

- established Islamic finance principles;
- scholarly disagreements;
- legal rules in Uzbekistan;
- practical financial realities;
- Ecofin’s own editorial judgment.

Where necessary, Ecofin will consult qualified scholars, Islamic finance practitioners, lawyers, economists, or other subject-matter experts.

---

## 4. Editorial Independence

Ecofin’s editorial content is independent.

Sponsors, donors, partner companies, universities, banks, financial institutions, or other organizations may not control our conclusions, suppress criticism, or require favorable coverage.

We may collaborate with institutions on educational content, but such collaborations must be clearly disclosed.

Ecofin will not publish hidden advertising. Paid, sponsored, or partner-supported content must be labeled clearly.

Editorial independence means:

- sponsors cannot approve or edit independent articles before publication;
- sponsors cannot require Ecofin to remove criticism;
- partnerships must be disclosed;
- financial relationships must not be hidden from readers;
- educational value must come before commercial value.

---

## 5. Sponsorship and Partnerships

Ecofin may accept sponsorships, donations, grants, institutional support, or commercial partnerships if they do not compromise our mission.

We will not accept sponsorships from categories that conflict with Ecofin’s ethics, including:

- betting and gambling companies;
- speculative trading platforms;
- predatory lending products;
- deceptive financial schemes;
- products or services that rely on financial harm to ordinary people;
- businesses whose main activity clearly conflicts with Islamic finance principles.

Ecofin may work with banks, universities, educational institutions, certification providers, companies, and public organizations when the collaboration serves educational value.

Sponsored content must disclose:

- who supported the content;
- whether the sponsor had any role in the topic selection;
- whether the sponsor reviewed factual descriptions about itself;
- whether Ecofin received money, services, or other support.

Sponsors may correct factual errors about their own products or operations, but they may not control Ecofin’s editorial judgment.

## 6. Accuracy and Sources

Ecofin aims to be accurate, but finance is complex and conditions change.

We use sources such as:

- official laws and regulations;
- Central Bank publications;
- official bank tariff pages and product terms;
- government statistics;
- international institutions;
- academic papers;
- company reports;
- public datasets;
- expert interviews;
- direct calculations from disclosed assumptions.

**Important factual claims must be supported by sources**. This is especially important for:

- interest rates;
- inflation numbers;
- loan terms;
- tax rules;
- legal claims;
- bank product comparisons;
- Islamic finance legal developments;
- statistics about Uzbekistan.

When we use calculations, we should disclose:

- the formula;
- the assumptions;
- the date of the data;
- the limits of the calculation.

When exact data is unavailable, we should say so clearly.

---

## 7. Calculators, Graphs, and Interactive Tools

Ecofin uses calculators, charts, and interactive tools to make finance easier to understand. Interactivity is at the center of Ecofin.

These tools are educational. They are not financial advice and may not reflect every fee, tax, penalty, bank condition, or legal detail.

Every major calculator should explain:

- what it calculates;
- what formula it uses;
- what assumptions it makes;
- what it does not include;
- when the result may be inaccurate.

Where possible, Ecofin will make calculator code open-source so readers can inspect, verify, and improve it.

---

## 8. Opinion and Analysis

Ecofin may publish opinionated analysis.

Opinionated content should still be fair, factual, and transparent. Strong conclusions are allowed, but they should be supported by reasoning and evidence.

We may criticize:

- harmful financial products;
- misleading advertising;
- poor financial habits;
- exploitative credit structures;
- weak public communication;
- speculative behavior;
- policies or products that harm ordinary people.

However, criticism should focus on facts, incentives, structures, and consequences. We should avoid personal attacks, insults, or careless accusations.

A strong article should be sharp without being reckless.

---

## 9. No Individual Financial Advice

Ecofin provides general financial education.

Our articles, calculators, videos, posts, and tools are not individual financial, legal, tax, investment, or religious advice. We acknowledge the complext reality and our own limitations.

Readers should consider their own circumstances and, where necessary, consult qualified professionals before making major decisions.

Ecofin may explain how a loan works, how inflation affects savings, or how to compare repayment schedules. But we do not tell individual readers exactly what to buy, sell, borrow, invest in, or sign.

---

## 10. Financial Product Coverage

Ecofin may discuss financial products such as loans, deposits, cards, insurance, payment systems, Islamic finance products, or investment instruments.

When covering financial products, we should aim to explain:

- how the product works;
- who benefits from it;
- what risks exist;
- what fees or penalties may apply;
- what the reader should check before using it;
- whether the product raises Islamic finance concerns.

Ecofin should not publish content that exists mainly to sell a financial product.

We do not publish affiliate-style recommendations for loans, speculative trading, betting, or similar products.

---

## 11. Corrections Policy

Ecofin will correct significant errors.

If a reader, expert, institution, or contributor identifies an error, we will review it. If the correction is valid, we will update the article.

For minor corrections, such as typos or formatting issues, we may edit the article without a public note.

For significant corrections, such as wrong formulas, wrong legal interpretation, wrong numbers, or misleading conclusions, we should add a correction note at the bottom of the article.

A correction note should include:

- what was wrong;
- what was changed;
- the date of correction;
- where appropriate, the source for the correction.

## 12. Updates and Outdated Content

Financial information changes.

Interest rates, inflation, laws, bank terms, tax rules, and product conditions may become outdated. Ecofin should show publication dates and, where relevant, last-updated dates.

For time-sensitive articles, we should include a note such as:

> This article uses data available as of [date]. Bank terms, laws, and rates may have changed since publication.

Important evergreen articles should be reviewed periodically, especially if they receive significant traffic.

---

## 13. Translation Policy

Ecofin aims to publish content in Uzbek, Russian, and English where possible.

Uzbek is the primary language because Ecofin’s main audience is in Uzbekistan.

Translations should preserve the meaning of the original article, but they do not need to be word-for-word literal. The priority is clarity for the reader in each language.

When laws, examples, jokes, or cultural references do not translate well, the translation may adapt them while preserving the core idea.

If there is a conflict between versions, the Uzbek version should be treated as the primary version unless stated otherwise.

---

## 14. Use of AI Tools

Ecofin may use AI tools for drafting, editing, translation, brainstorming, code assistance, summaries, and formatting.

AI-generated output must not be published without human review.

For factual, legal, financial, or religious claims, AI tools are not sufficient as a source. Such claims must be checked against reliable sources or expert review where appropriate.

Ecofin remains responsible for all published content, whether AI tools were used or not.

## 15. Contributor Standards

Ecofin may accept contributions from writers, developers, translators, designers, researchers, students, professionals, and subject-matter experts.

Contributors should follow this editorial policy.

Contributors must disclose conflicts of interest, including:

- employment by a company discussed in the article;
- paid relationships with a company or institution;
- affiliate or referral relationships;
- personal financial interest in a product or topic;
- institutional pressure to present something favorably.

Ecofin may edit contributed work for clarity, structure, accuracy, tone, and consistency.

Publication is not guaranteed.

---

## 16. Expert Review

Some topics require extra care.

Ecofin should seek expert review where practical for articles involving:

- Islamic finance rulings;
- legal interpretation;
- taxation;
- banking regulation;
- investment products;
- macroeconomic claims;
- complex financial formulas;
- institutional accusations;
- health, legal, or livelihood-sensitive advice.

Expert review does not mean the expert controls Ecofin’s editorial position. It means Ecofin tries to reduce mistakes before publication.

---

## 17. Conflicts of Interest

Ecofin must disclose meaningful conflicts of interest.

A conflict of interest does not automatically prevent publication, but it must be visible to readers when it could affect trust.

Examples:

- A bank sponsors a series about banking.
- A university supports a series about its programs.
- A company provides data used in an article.
- A contributor works for an institution mentioned in the article.
- Ecofin receives payment for a workshop, series, tool, or partnership.

Disclosure should be clear and placed where readers can see it.

---

## 18. Privacy and User Data

Ecofin respects reader privacy.

Our educational content should not require readers to reveal sensitive personal financial data. If Ecofin offers tools that collect or process data, we should explain:

- what data is collected;
- where it is stored;
- whether it leaves the user’s device;
- whether it is shared;
- how users can delete it.

For local-first tools, Ecofin should clearly explain that user data stays on the device unless the user chooses to export, sync, or share it.

Ecofin should avoid collecting unnecessary personal data.

## 19. Style Guide

Ecofin’s writing should be:

- clear;
- practical;
- direct;
- locally relevant;
- respectful;
- evidence-based;
- visually supported where useful.

We should avoid:

- empty motivational writing;
- vague “financial freedom” promises;
- unnecessary jargon;
- exaggerated claims;
- fear-based marketing;
- clickbait;
- hidden advertising;
- personal attacks;
- unsupported statistics;
- pretending certainty where uncertainty exists.

A good Ecofin article should answer:

1. What is the concept?
2. Why does it matter in real life?
3. How does it work mathematically or practically?
4. What is the local Uzbek context?
5. What mistakes should readers avoid?
6. What should readers calculate or check?
7. What sources support the article?

---

## 20. Editorial Workflow

Ecofin’s standard editorial workflow should be:

1. Choose a topic based on reader value and strategic relevance.
2. Create an outline.
3. Collect sources and examples.
4. Draft the article.
5. Add formulas, calculators, charts, or visuals where useful.
6. Check factual claims.
7. Review tone and clarity.
8. Add disclaimers and source links.
9. Translate where appropriate.
10. Publish.
11. Monitor feedback.
12. Correct or update when necessary.

For series-based content, each article should connect to the previous and next article where possible.

---

## 21. Series-First Publishing

Ecofin prioritizes complete educational series over scattered standalone posts.

A series should help the reader move from basic understanding to practical decision-making. Each series should have:

- a clear topic;
- a target reader;
- a logical order;
- a hub page;
- internal links between articles;
- practical examples;
- interactive tools where useful;
- citations and sources;
- a final summary or checklist.

Ecofin’s early editorial focus is to build a strong library of high-quality series before expanding into video, podcasts, or other formats.

## 22. Editorial Disclaimer

Ecofin’s content is for educational purposes only.

We do not provide individual financial, investment, legal, tax, or religious advice. Financial decisions involve risk, and readers are responsible for their own decisions.

Ecofin tries to provide accurate and useful information, but we cannot guarantee that every article, calculator, or tool reflects the latest laws, rates, fees, or market conditions.

Where a decision is important, readers should check official sources and consult qualified professionals.
