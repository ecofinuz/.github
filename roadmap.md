# Roadmap for Ecofin

Ecofin is a free, open-source project that aims to bring open-source to personal finance and financial literacy. I aim to consolidate many facets of financial literacy into one central repository, Ecofin: open-source desktop financial tracker, economics + financial blog, Instagram/YouTube/TikTok shorts, long-form YouTube content, series-based learning, and many interactive financial tools (budgeting, calculators, graphs, open-data).

In the far future, Ecofin will produce video lessons for an online video platform, Ulus. This is a possible idea that needs vetting.

## Philosophy and Ethics

Finance is a very important topic in the lives of ordinary Uzbeks. We have deep systematic issues with financial literacy. I still get calls from friends asking for a loan so that they can pay their interest. I presume you have heard of people commiting suicide due to 1xbet. Getting into debt due to day trading. A young groom going to Russia to pay off wedding debt.

This is harmful for ordinary people. To contribute to these issues, we will be writing not only a knowledge guide, but also give practical advice. ChatGPT can write an excellent generic post, but we will be writing actionable insights, taking the Uzbek context into account.

Ecofin will be bound by Islamic finance laws (Sharia law). We do not advertise, nor endorse, nor encourage haram parts of contemporary finance: interest, guaranteed income without risk-sharing, betting, speculation, creative accounting, and etc.

Therefore, Ecofin is not merely an Uzbek Wikipedia for financial literacy; it is an opinionated financial literacy company guided by Islamic finance. Our business will be bound by natural and divine law.

## Company Structure

Ecofin will be a for-profit company, not a non-profit. Being a non-profit severely restrics how we raise and spend money for operations and expansions. Being a for-profit, independent company will allow us to act independently, pay out contributors freely, and invest in projects without asking for permission from our supporters.

Ecofin is my idea, and I don't look forward to being bound by non-profit laws/donaters on how I spend money on potential ideas and on myself.

Also, a for-profit organization allows making money for its founders/employees/contributors. There is nothing wrong with that.

## Revenue

The Ecofin itself will be free for users, forever. The way to make money will be to make a partnership with enterprises. Financial literacy and education are systematic issues, and companies will most likely pursue partnerships for good reputation, especially banks. However, we need a very strong reputation for that.

This model comes from the programming world, open-source world to be exact. Many open-source, beneficial projects are supported and sponsored through partnerships and contributions. Here is an [example](https://www.djangoproject.com/fundraising/#:~:text=Seoul%2C%20South%20Korea-,DSF%20Supporters,-Our%20donors%20make).

This business model is relatively new; the other way, making users pay for content and our products, does not work. Many have tried (and failed) that way. We will be swimming in uncharted waters.

Ecofin will be a for-profit, yet open-source company that generates revenue through partnerships and donations.

## Desktop Application

Initially, I tried to make an online web platform for all things personal finance: budgeting, tracking, categorizing, useful insights, etc. However, this territory is filled with AI solutions (Chatgpt Finance), and many other paid online tools, and Excel templates.

More importantly though, these are the services I would not use myself. Men in Uzbekistan are very sensitive about their finances. Asking them to pay and input their expenses are very likely to fail to generate a critical base.

Therefore, I decided to pursue the open-source route: a free, local desktop application with no trackers, no monitoring, and no spyware. Indeed, this will be a local desktop app with no centralized database. No accounts on the server.

This approach is also new and controversial, but worth pursuing. It is interesting for its own technological sake.

## Blog Post Series

The blog, blog.ecofin.uz, is now based on the series on a particular topic, such as investing, loans, savings, gold, economics, etc. This format allows us to dive deep into topics and provide valuable long-form content for the reader.

The difference is, we place a heavy emphasis on interactivity: graphs, charts, calculators, visuals. Like [this](https://blog.ecofin.uz/kredit/sodda-va-murakkab-foizlar/#interaktiv-grafik) and [that](https://blog.ecofin.uz/kredit/kredit-qanday-ishlaydi/#annuitet).

Each blog post is translated into three dominant languages within Uzbekistan: Uzbek, Russian, and English.

The series approach is relatively new; we can take it far. With enough series, this can be a database of its own.

## Specific university/company/accredition series + SEO

As Ecofin starts to gain traction, I plan to collaborate with universities and big finance companies to write series on their own courses/operations. For example, Westminster has 4-6 Bachelor's on economics/finance and several Master's courses. This university would gain a lot by writing posts on their courses. This not only highlights them, but also gives them presence online.

The same goes for companies. For Big4, banks, and big commercial enterprises. Their departments, their internship programs, hiring process, and etc. The exact content to be written will be determined in the process.

We can also collaborate with ACCA/CFA/CFI/GMAT teachers to write content on their certitifactions/tests on Ecofin. Free advertising and presence for them, while we gain further content and traction.

A win-win for both sides of the collaboration.

Technically, this involves SEO (Search Engine Optimization). This collaboration will only be possible when Ecofin starts ranking within the first page at Google/Yandex. Meaning, the content written on our site should be of high quality and relevance.

I started reading Product-led SEO and will further read the Art of SEO in the coming months.

## Shorts/Reels/YouTube

This is a long-shot. But if/when we get revenue, I plan to hire a team (video editor, shooter, speaker) to shoot short-term videos for Instagram, YouTube, and possibly TikTok. This idea needs a lot of vetting.

Similarly, long-term YouTube podcasts and interviews are in our radar, but it needs a very strong and mature finance professional and a big budget.

## WebFinance

In conclusion, I do not indent to make Ecofin yet another generic guide. The zenith of Ecofin will be when it transitions to WebFinance: a central financial repository for Uzbekistan: guides, blog posts, video lessons, interactive finance charts, financial data, reports, podcasts, applications.

Honestly, I have no idea how far this idea can go. Actually, no one does. But Ecofin is not a pipe dream: it is a potential idea that takes a very different approach: open-source.

As far as I know, no one has tried building a business in the open-source in Uzbekistan. Many have succeeded abroad in the IT sphere: GitLab, Wordpress, MongoDB, and others.

I will seriously pursue this idea. It looks cool and interesting.
